Our Coaching Process

1

Discovery & Observation (Week 1)

I spend 3–4 hours observing your classroom and interviewing you about the challenges you're facing, what you've already tried, and your goals.

2

Behavior Plan Development (Week 2)

Within 1–2 business days, I deliver a custom behavior support plan that identifies the function of the behavior and provides specific antecedent and consequence strategies.

3

Implementation & Coaching (Months 1–3+)

We observe, coach, model strategies, and collect data. I provide specific feedback and support until you're implementing with confidence and fidelity.

4

Fading & Independence (Month 3+)

As you gain competence, we systematically reduce coaching frequency. You succeed independently while data shows sustained behavior improvement.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does coaching typically last?

Most engagements run 3–6 months. We start with intensive coaching (1–2x per week) and fade as you demonstrate fidelity and see behavior improvements. The timeline depends on your progress.

What if I don't see results quickly?

We track data every session. If a strategy isn't working after 2–3 weeks of implementation, we adjust the plan. Behavior change takes time, but we move quickly to find what works for your students.

Can you work with multiple teachers at once?

Absolutely. Many schools work with 2–3 teachers under a single contract. I stagger observations and coaching to be efficient while giving each teacher the support they need.

Do you work with students with IEPs?

Yes. In fact, many students on IEPs benefit from behavior support plans. We coordinate with your special education team and align with existing IEP goals.

What if behavior gets worse before it gets better?

Sometimes behavior temporarily increases when we remove reinforcement for problem behavior. This is called an extinction burst and is normal. We monitor closely and adjust as needed.

How is progress measured?

We use objective data collection (ABC logs, frequency counts, duration) to track behavior changes. Monthly progress reports show baseline data, current trends, and whether we're hitting our goals.
